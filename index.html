Founder’s Story

Grace Chilongo – Founder, YEEM

YEEM was born out of a deeply personal journey, one shaped by uncertainty, resilience, and purpose.

When I was selected to attend university, it was a dream come true. But that dream was quickly threatened by financial challenges. At one point, I was on the verge of withdrawing, unsure if I would ever return to continue my education. During my first semester break, with no clear path forward, I felt completely lost.

In that moment, after exhausting all efforts to secure support from different organisations, a friend encouraged me to try Liwonde National Park. While others had already returned to school, I was still searching for a way forward. I decided to take that step, not knowing what to expect. By grace, I received assistance—but more importantly, I received words that would shape my purpose forever.

“It is not African Parks paying your tuition fees, it is the wildlife, the trees, and the environment that are investing in your future.”

Those words stayed with me. I realised that my education was not just a personal achievement, it was a responsibility. A responsibility to give back to the environment, and to communities facing similar challenges.

That moment marked the beginning of Youth Empowerment and Environmental Management (YEEM).

Growing up in a context where girls’ education was often sidelined, I understood firsthand the barriers many young people face. YEEM was created as a platform to empower youth, support communities, and promote environmental stewardship.

During one of our general meetings as African Park Liwonde (APL) scholars, I shared this vision. It was embraced, especially by Chiyembekezo Wilson, who has remained a pillar of the organisation.

Today, YEEM stands as a reflection of that journey, from uncertainty to purpose. A platform where young people lead change, communities build resilience, and environmental responsibility is lived every day.

When the environment invests in us, we must invest back in it, and in each other.

Mission

To empower youth and communities to address environmental challenges through sustainable solutions.

Vision

A sustainable future where young people lead in solving environmental challenges.

Objectives

  • Youth Empowerment and Leadership: Equip young people with the necessary skills and opportunities to lead in sustainable development, focusing on their participation in economic, social, and environmental sectors.
  • Climate Resilience and Adaptation: Build climate-resilient communities by training youth in disaster preparedness, sustainable agriculture, and water management, ensuring they can respond effectively to climate-related risks.
  • Digital Innovation for Sustainability: Integrate digital tools and emerging technologies into environmental problem-solving, offering youth skills in data analysis, GIS, and tech-driven solutions to address climate challenges.
  • Protection of Biodiversity and Ecosystems: Promote the conservation of biodiversity and the restoration of ecosystems through youth-led initiatives that combine traditional knowledge with innovative practices.
  • Inclusive Community Development: Foster the inclusion of youth, women, and men in economic, social, and technological structures to drive sustainable and equitable development.
  • Youth-Led Policy Advocacy: Empower young people to engage in policy advocacy, shaping local, national, and global environmental policies to promote sustainability, conservation, and climate action.
  • Global Collaboration and Cultural Exchange: Facilitate cross-cultural partnerships and global collaborations to expand the impact of youth-led initiatives, fostering shared learning and global solidarity in environmental action.
